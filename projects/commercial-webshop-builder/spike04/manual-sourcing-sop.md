# Manual Sourcing SOP

## Input gate

Start only when:

- core vehicle and part information is present;
- consent is sufficient for sourcing;
- no private requester information needs to be shared;
- the case is not already blocked by missing information.

## Step 1 — Prepare the case

Create a privacy-safe summary containing only the information needed to identify the part:

```text
vehicle make and model
year or period
requested part
known variant, marking or part number
condition or originality preference
known fitment uncertainty
```

## Step 2 — Check missing information

At minimum check:

- make, model and year or generation;
- exact part type;
- left, right, front, rear or other position;
- complete unit versus component;
- photo, marking, measurement or part number;
- original, reproduction, used or new preference;
- condition;
- related mounting or fitment parts;
- market or vehicle variant where relevant.

Stop and request clarification when missing information makes lead assessment unreliable.

## Step 3 — Build search terms

Use:

- exact known description;
- alternative names and translations;
- make, model and year or generation;
- part number or marking;
- condition terms;
- related fitment terms;
- specialist terminology discovered during the run.

## Step 4 — Check channels

Use a bounded sequence selected for the case:

1. general indexed search;
2. marketplaces;
3. specialist catalogues or suppliers;
4. owner clubs, forums or communities;
5. breakers or other specialist sources when justified.

Do not share requester contact details.

## Step 5 — Refine the specification

When a source reveals better terminology, fitment, measurements, a part number, a generation or a related component, update the case and retry only the most relevant earlier channels.

See [specification-refinement-loop.md](specification-refinement-loop.md).

## Step 6 — Score outcomes

| Score | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 1 | irrelevant |
| 2 | possible but too vague |
| 3 | relevant route with material uncertainty |
| 4 | strong likely route or match requiring confirmation |
| 5 | strongly supported match with sufficient identifying and availability evidence |

A specialist route is not automatically a product lead.

## Step 7 — Log aggregate evidence

Record:

- safe run label;
- active time;
- channels checked;
- best score;
- number and type of useful outcomes;
- missing information;
- final next action.

Do not place contact logs or private tracking data in the canonical dossier.

## Stop rules

Stop when:

- a strong result has been identified;
- missing information blocks reliable assessment;
- the timebox is reached;
- the defined channel set is exhausted;
- consent or privacy is unclear.
